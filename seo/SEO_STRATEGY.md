## PART 1 — SITE IDENTITY

**Site name:** MindWave JA
**Domain:** mindwaveja.com
**One-sentence description:** Free business-launch playbooks for Jamaican entrepreneurs who want to build real income streams — 79 phase packs covering food, beauty, retail, events, digital, and more, plus artist digital territory licensing for Jamaican music icons.
**Primary conversion goal:** Phase pack access — visitor finds a pack relevant to their business idea and bookmarks/shares/uses it
**Secondary conversion goal:** ADTL Artist Site inquiry — musician contacts MindWave JA to claim or create their digital territory
**Site stage:** Live — SEO infrastructure needs to be built on top of existing content

**Niche:**
Jamaican entrepreneurship enablement — free, detailed, ground-level business playbooks built specifically for the Jamaican economic environment. Not generic startup advice. Not US/UK-centric business content. Every pack is calibrated to JA costs, JA market behaviour, JA time/transport constraints, and JA cultural norms. Sits at the intersection of practical business education and cultural specificity.

**ADTL sub-niche:** Caribbean digital identity infrastructure for music artists — owning your territory online as a reggae/dancehall artist, distinct from social media dependence.

**Niche depth:** Deep for JA audience; underserved globally
**Competitive density:** Low — no direct competitor does JA-specific business packs at this depth and for free

**What this site is NOT:**
- Not a generic "how to start a business" blog
- Not a loan/grant platform
- Not a US or UK business resource adapted for Jamaica
- Not a social media marketing agency
- Not a music streaming platform

**Keywords we will never chase:**
- Generic "how to make money online" (no specific JA angle)
- "Start a business in America" / "UK startup"
- Generic "business ideas" without JA modifier
- Music streaming / spotify plays

**Audience we explicitly exclude:**
- People looking for get-rich-quick schemes (packs are honest about effort required)
- Non-Jamaican entrepreneurs who want generic advice
- Artists who want social media management only (ADTL is digital territory, not content management)

## PART 2 — MARKET ARCHITECTURE

### 2A — Primary Target Market

**Market name:** The JA Builder

**One-line description:** The Jamaican man or woman who has a viable skill or business idea but has never seen a structured playbook built for their actual market conditions — not American templates, not theoretical MBA frameworks.

---

**ARCHETYPE NAME:** The JA Builder

**CORE MOTIVATION:**
They want to build something real. Not because they saw a YouTube video about passive income — because they have a skill, a hustle, or a gap they've spotted in their community, and they want to turn it into consistent money. They're not waiting for someone to give them a chance. But they've never had a structured system that tells them exactly what to do for their specific idea in their specific market. MindWave JA packs are that system.

**CURRENT PAIN STATE:**
They have an idea but don't know where to start — what equipment to buy, what to charge, how to get first customers, how to avoid the traps. They've searched online but everything they find is American. The prices are wrong, the market references are wrong, the tone is wrong. They feel like the information that exists wasn't built for them. Some have already tried and failed because of a gap (underpriced, wrong supplier, no marketing plan). They don't want to fail again.

**ASPIRATION STATE:**
A clear, step-by-step guide that tells them exactly what to start, what it costs in JMD, what their daily minimum should be, how to scale, and what to do when things go wrong — all calibrated to Jamaica. They want to read a pack and say "this is exactly my situation" — and start the next day.

**TRUST SIGNALS:**
- JMD prices (not USD), real supplier references
- Acknowledges Jamaica-specific obstacles (transport, light bills, cash economy)
- Written in a direct, no-fluff tone — not corporate, not patronizing
- Content that reflects lived Jamaican experience (beauty culture, event culture, food culture)
- Free — no paywall, no subscription

**VOCABULARY IN PAIN STATE:**
*(how they search when stuck)*
- "how to start a business in Jamaica"
- "business ideas Jamaica 2025"
- "how to make money in Jamaica with little capital"
- "how to start a food business in Jamaica"
- "how to start a beauty business Jamaica"
- "small business ideas Jamaica"
- "how to start a lash business Jamaica"
- "how to sell food in Jamaica legally"
- "cleaning service Jamaica how to start"
- "mobile business Jamaica"
- "business plan Jamaica"

**VOCABULARY IN ASPIRATION STATE:**
*(what they search when hopeful)*
- "successful small business Jamaica"
- "how to scale a small business Jamaica"
- "Jamaica entrepreneur resources"
- "JA entrepreneur support"
- "business playbook Jamaica"
- "how to earn consistent income Jamaica"

**HOW THEY SEARCH:**
- Mobile-first, often mid-day or late evening
- Short aggressive queries + some long conversational ("how much does it cost to start a lash business in Jamaica")
- Mix of Patois thinking, standard English typing
- Rarely uses quotation marks
- Searches in bursts when the idea is fresh — then comes back months later

**WHAT THEY CLICK ON:**
- Results that name their specific business type or industry
- Headlines with JMD pricing or specific Jamaica references
- Lists and step-by-step guides
- Avoid: anything that sounds like it was written outside Jamaica

**WHAT MAKES THEM LEAVE:**
- USD pricing without JMD conversion
- Generic advice that ignores JA-specific constraints
- No proof of understanding the Jamaican market
- Slow load on mobile
- Paywalls or email-gate before they can read

---

**Demographic snapshot:**
- Age: 19–45
- Geography: Kingston, St. Andrew, Montego Bay, Spanish Town, rural parishes — nationwide
- Diaspora: UK, US, Canada — searching for JA-specific info for relatives or return-migration plans
- Language: English + Patois code-switching
- Platforms: WhatsApp (discovery via sharing), YouTube, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok
- Income: JMD 30K–150K/month range — limited capital, looking for low-startup-cost ideas
- Education: CXC level and above; not necessarily tertiary educated but self-driven learners

### 2B — Adjacent Markets

**ADJACENT MARKET 1: The JA Diaspora Planner**

**Name:** The Return Builder
**Relationship to primary:** Same cultural knowledge, same market curiosity, but geographically distant and planning ahead — wants to understand the JA business landscape for a return move or remote investment
**Key difference from primary:** Not in Jamaica right now; may have USD capital; less urgency, more research mode

**Psychological profile:**
- Core motivation: Build something back home before or after returning to Jamaica
- Pain vocabulary: "how to invest in Jamaica from abroad", "starting a business in Jamaica from UK", "Jamaica small business from diaspora", "what business can I start in Jamaica with savings"
- Aspiration vocabulary: "successful Jamaica business diaspora", "move back to Jamaica and work for yourself"
- How they search: Longer queries, research mode, mobile + desktop, often evening after work

**Content overlap:** All phase packs are relevant; capital-intensive packs (retail, events) may land better with diaspora than local audience who has less startup capital

**What we won't build for them:** UK/US tax or legal content. The site is JA-focused. Diaspora serves themselves by reading JA packs; we don't adapt the content outward.

---

**ADJACENT MARKET 2: The JA Artist (ADTL)**

**Name:** The Territory Builder
**Relationship to primary:** Same Jamaican cultural root, different product — they're not looking for business packs; they want to own their digital space as a music artist
**Key difference from primary:** Music-industry context; already has a following; wants digital infrastructure, not a business playbook

**Psychological profile:**
- Core motivation: Build an independent digital home for their fanbase — not dependent on Instagram/TikTok staying alive
- Pain vocabulary: "artist website Jamaica", "how to build a website for a reggae artist", "own my digital space", "reggae artist website builder", "independent music website Jamaica"
- Aspiration vocabulary: "own my territory online", "artist website with merch", "ADTL license", "MindWave artist site"
- How they search: Mobile; often sent directly by word-of-mouth or the MindWave JA team

**Content overlap:** The ADTL marketplace page and artist roster page serve this market entirely

**What we won't build for them:** Generic music distribution or social media management. ADTL is digital territory infrastructure only.

---

**ADJACENT MARKET 3: The JA Educator / Skills Trainer**

**Name:** The Multiplier
**Relationship to primary:** Uses or recommends MindWave JA packs as a teaching resource — youth workers, HEART Trust instructors, school counsellors, MSME advisors
**Key difference from primary:** Not building a business themselves; looking for structured curriculum they can use with students or clients

**Psychological profile:**
- Core motivation: Find structured, culturally relevant business content to teach
- Pain vocabulary: "business resources Jamaica for youth", "entrepreneur curriculum Jamaica", "free business education Jamaica", "MSME support Jamaica small business"
- Aspiration vocabulary: "Jamaica entrepreneurship resources teachers", "business pack download Jamaica"
- How they search: Desktop, longer queries, often comes from organizational context

**Content overlap:** Every pack, especially the free PDF/print view if implemented

**What we won't build for them:** Formal curriculum or certification content. The packs teach by being used, not by being adapted into syllabi.

### 3D — Dialect and Code-Switch Variants

**Primary language of search:** Standard English (JA-inflected vocabulary)

**Patois / code-switch patterns:**
JA builders search mostly in standard English but the queries carry Caribbean rhythm and reference points.

| Standard English query | Jamaican-inflected version |
|---|---|
| "how to start a business in Jamaica" | "how fi start a business in Jamaica" |
| "business ideas Jamaica" | "biz ideas Jamaica" / "hustle ideas Jamaica" |
| "how to make money Jamaica" | "how fi mek money in Jamaica" |
| "sell food Jamaica" | "cook and sell Jamaica" / "hustle food JA" |

**Code-switch implications:**
- `fi` constructions appear in longer conversational queries — worth including in on-page body text to match the vocabulary naturally
- "hustle" is preferred over "business" in many low-formality contexts — both terms should appear in content
- On-page content should carry Jamaican English rhythm in headers and intros — not full Patois, but recognizably local voice

## PART 4 — CONTENT ARCHITECTURE

### 4A — Content Pillars

**PILLAR 1: Phase Packs (the core product)**
- Topic: All 79 free business playbooks for Jamaican entrepreneurs
- Why this site owns it: No one else has this content. Every pack is original, JA-specific, and free.
- Primary archetype: The JA Builder
- Pillar page URL: `/phase-packs`
- Format: Index page with category filters + individual pack pages at `/phase-packs/[slug]`

**PILLAR 2: Business Guides by Industry (JA-specific)**
- Topic: Deep-dive guides per industry vertical — beauty, food, retail, digital, events, cleaning
- Why: Allows the site to rank for high-intent "how to start X in Jamaica" queries beyond just pack names
- Primary archetype: The JA Builder + Diaspora
- Pillar page URLs: `/guide/beauty-businesses-jamaica`, `/guide/food-businesses-jamaica`, etc.
- Format: Long-form guide (1200–2000 words) linking to relevant packs

**PILLAR 3: Artist Digital Territory (ADTL)**
- Topic: What ADTL is, the 20 owned domains, how to claim or create an artist site
- Why: Only source for this specific offering
- Primary archetype: The Territory Builder (artist market)
- Pillar page URL: `/marketplace/artist-digital-territory-license`
- Format: Product/offering page — artist names, domain list, pricing, CTA

**PILLAR 4: The JA Entrepreneur Resource Hub**
- Topic: Jamaica economic context — regulations, costs, market conditions — grounded in `JA_ECONOMIC_CLIMATE.md`
- Why: Higher-authority content that earns backlinks from institutions, NGOs, press
- Pillar page URL: `/resources`
- Format: Hub page linking to pack categories + external references

### 4D — Content Tone

**Primary archetype tone:**
- Formality: Direct, plain English with Jamaican rhythm — not formal, not condescending
- Language: "you" address, present tense, specific to Jamaica
- Sentence length: Short-to-medium — no academic padding
- JA references: Natural — "transport cost", "light bill", "market day", "Kingston / St. Andrew"
- Never: Motivational slogans. "In this guide, we will..." intros. Generic hustle culture language.

**ADTL / artist tone:**
- More elevated — cultural significance language ("own your territory", "your digital sovereign space")
- Still direct — facts about domains, pricing, what's included
- Not corporate — this is for artists, the voice should carry creative energy

## PART 5 — TECHNICAL SEO

### 5A — Current State

Next.js 16 with SSR = good base. Pages are server-rendered, crawlable by default. No SPA blocker issue (unlike whatissolob.com). Main gaps are:

- **Metadata**: Most pages have minimal or no per-page title/description
- **Structured data**: No schema markup on any page
- **Internal linking**: Pack pages exist but cross-linking between related packs is thin
- **Sitemap**: May exist (Next.js can auto-generate) — needs verification
- **OG images**: Absent or generic
